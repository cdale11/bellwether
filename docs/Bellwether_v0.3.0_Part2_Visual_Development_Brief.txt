BELLWETHER v0.3.0 — PART 2 VISUAL DEVELOPMENT BRIEF

Version: Planning Draft 1 Purpose: Visual Style Exploration, Asset
Bible, and First Integrated Visual Vertical Slice

1. PURPOSE

Part 2 establishes Bellwether’s coherent visual identity and the
production system for future visual assets.

Bellwether remains a text-first, state-driven village life RPG. Visuals
support prose, place, character presence, emotional attachment, seasonal
familiarity, and later supernatural contradiction. The game should not
become a conventional visual novel in which images replace simulation or
prose.

The visual direction is:

-   warm pastoral life-sim at the beginning;
-   rural English village identity with a relatively modern but
    temporally ambiguous 21st-century feeling;
-   anime and visual-novel character presentation;
-   some Stardew Valley influence in warmth, seasonal attachment,
    recurring places, and readable visual identity;
-   restrained Disco Elysium influence in psychology, subjective
    perception, investigation, and later visual instability;
-   gradual movement from comforting, vibrant normality toward darker,
    stranger, less trustworthy presentation.

The goal of Part 2 is not mass asset generation. The goal is to
establish a repeatable visual language before scaling production.

2. DEVELOPMENT SEQUENCE

Part 2 should proceed in this order:

1.  Visual style exploration.
2.  Selection of the final Bellwether art direction.
3.  Asset Bible creation.
4.  Canonical character sheets.
5.  Canonical location sheets.
6.  Controlled vertical-slice asset production.
7.  Scene-stage integration.
8.  Character sprite and portrait integration.
9.  Weather, time, and seasonal presentation tests.
10. Consistency review.
11. Diagnostic and screenshot certification.
12. Only after certification: scaled asset production.

No large asset library should be generated before the style and
consistency rules are approved.

3. VISUAL STYLE EXPLORATION

The first task is to create a small comparison set showing the same
Bellwether subject matter in several candidate interpretations.

The comparison should use consistent subject matter:

-   Ashcroft Cottage and garden;
-   an ordinary warm daytime scene;
-   one rain variant;
-   Mara as the initial character test;
-   one ordinary neutral expression;
-   one warm expression;
-   one guarded or concerned expression.

Candidate directions to compare:

Direction A — Soft Painted Rural Anime

Characteristics: - painterly backgrounds; - natural vegetation; - warm
atmospheric light; - detailed but not photorealistic architecture; -
expressive anime characters with restrained proportions; - strong
weather mood; - suitable for comfort-to-horror contrast.

Potential strength: Best balance of place, warmth, VN presentation, and
later atmospheric horror.

Direction B — Clean Modern Visual Novel

Characteristics: - cleaner linework; - sharper character rendering; -
simpler environmental painting; - clearer sprites and portraits; -
stronger dialogue-focused presentation.

Potential strength: Highly readable character interaction and efficient
expression production.

Potential weakness: May make the village feel more like a VN backdrop
than a living simulated place.

Direction C — Storybook Anime Life-Sim

Characteristics: - softer shapes; - slightly stylized proportions; -
warm colours; - approachable environments; - strong seasonal charm; -
gentler ordinary-life presentation.

Potential strength: Makes early Bellwether highly inviting and increases
contrast when horror corrupts it.

Potential weakness: Must avoid becoming visually childish or overly
whimsical.

Direction D — Textured Psychological Rural Illustration

Characteristics: - anime-influenced characters; - more textured and
subjective backgrounds; - selective painterly distortion; - stronger
Disco Elysium influence; - unusual lighting and psychological
atmosphere.

Potential strength: Excellent for psychology and horror.

Potential weakness: May weaken the required comforting ordinary-life
baseline if used too strongly from the beginning.

The preferred final direction may combine elements, but it must be
defined clearly enough to reproduce consistently.

Current provisional recommendation: Direction A as the foundation, with
selected warmth from Direction C and restrained psychological texture
from Direction D only when state justifies it.

4. ASSET BIBLE

After style selection, create a permanent Bellwether Asset Bible.

The Asset Bible must define:

Character rules

-   canonical face shape;
-   approximate age;
-   height relationships;
-   body proportions;
-   hairstyle;
-   hair colour;
-   eye colour;
-   skin tone;
-   recurring clothing;
-   seasonal clothing variants;
-   work clothing where relevant;
-   accessories;
-   posture;
-   gesture vocabulary;
-   expression vocabulary;
-   injury presentation;
-   psychological deterioration rules;
-   supernatural corruption rules;
-   recurrence-recognition rules.

Architecture rules

-   building materials;
-   window placement;
-   door placement;
-   roof shape;
-   chimneys;
-   fences;
-   garden layout;
-   recurring signs;
-   road and path materials;
-   village furniture;
-   utility infrastructure;
-   modern objects allowed in the setting;
-   objects that would make the setting feel too specifically dated.

A recurring building must remain recognizably the same building
across: - clear weather; - rain; - snow; - different seasons; - day and
night; - supernatural variants.

Environmental rules

-   vegetation by seasonal phase;
-   ground state;
-   crop appearance;
-   tree species vocabulary;
-   hedge and field language;
-   wildlife visibility;
-   cloud treatment;
-   rain treatment;
-   snow accumulation;
-   storm treatment;
-   mist depth;
-   seasonal palette;
-   time-of-day lighting.

Composition rules

-   standard scene-card aspect ratio;
-   camera height;
-   typical focal length feeling;
-   character scale in scene;
-   horizon placement;
-   safe areas for UI overlays;
-   dialogue portrait crop;
-   sprite crop;
-   mobile-safe composition.

Horror rules

Ordinary assets establish normality first.

Supernatural visual changes must be: - state-driven; - authored or
bounded; - recognizable as violations of learned normality; - rare at
first; - increasingly aggressive only under justified supernatural
pressure.

Avoid meaningless random glitches.

Part 2 should initially cover only a small representative set.

Locations

1.  Ashcroft Cottage exterior.
2.  Ashcroft Cottage interior.
3.  Cottage Garden.
4.  Village Green.

Core characters

Background population

6. CHARACTER PRESENTATION SYSTEM

Core characters should have two related presentation forms.

Scene presence

A character may appear in the scene stage through: - sprite; - half-body
figure; - controlled composited character layer; - specially authored
scene composition for important moments.

Dialogue presentation

Conversation mode should use expressive VN-style portrait or bust
presentation.

Initial expression family for Mara and Jonah: - neutral; - warm; -
amused; - concerned; - guarded; - tired; - frightened; - angry.

Later states may include: - injured; - psychologically affected; -
subtle supernatural influence; - recurrence recognition.

Expression selection must use authoritative state and bounded
presentation logic. It should not rely only on naive sentiment analysis
of the latest line.

Relevant inputs may include: - conversation context; - relationship
state; - recent social events; - current activity; - injury; -
psychological condition; - anomaly exposure; - recurrence echo.

7. SCENE RESOLUTION MODEL

Do not create one unique image for every possible combination.

Use a compositional system:

Base Location + Seasonal Base Variant + Time-of-Day Treatment + Weather
Treatment + Character Presence + Event Modifier + Supernatural Modifier

Example:

Cottage Garden + Early Spring + Late Afternoon + Light Rain + Mara
Present + No Village Event Modifier + No Supernatural Corruption

Important states may receive genuinely authored alternate artwork.

Examples: - snow-covered Ashcroft Cottage; - storm-damaged Village
Green; - major story confrontation; - severe supernatural architecture
corruption.

Less extreme states can use controlled layered treatments.

8. UI INTEGRATION PRINCIPLES

The scene stage built in v0.3.0 Part 1 is the target surface for Part 2
assets.

Part 2 should integrate: - scene-card resolver; - asset manifest; -
fallback handling; - portrait resolver; - expression resolver; -
character-presence resolver; - weather treatment; - time-of-day
treatment; - responsive image cropping; - mobile-safe focal positioning.

The frontend must never invent authoritative game state from artwork.

If Mara is not present according to simulation state, her visual layer
must not appear merely because an attractive scene variant contains her.

Likewise, visual weather must match authoritative weather unless a
deliberate supernatural contradiction is active.

9. PLAYER CHARACTER PRESENTATION

A decision is still required during Part 2 implementation:

Option A: Player character remains visually off-screen, preserving
first-person identification.

Option B: Player receives a canonical visual design used selectively in
major scenes, mirrors, recurrence sequences, and special illustrations.

Provisional recommendation: Use Option A for ordinary play and reserve
any player-character visual representation for carefully authored
exceptional scenes. This preserves text-RPG identification and avoids
forcing constant avatar presentation.

10. AUDIO BOUNDARY

Part 2 should prepare visual components to cooperate with later audio
work but should not attempt full audio implementation.

Scene metadata may expose: - location ambience category; - weather
ambience category; - indoor/outdoor state; - ecological ambience
category; - supernatural audio suppression or distortion flags.

Full ambient audio belongs primarily to v0.3.0 Part 3.

Part 2 testing must include technical and visual checks.

Technical

-   JavaScript syntax validation;
-   asset manifest validation;
-   missing-asset fallback;
-   scene resolver correctness;
-   portrait resolver correctness;
-   authoritative NPC presence matching;
-   weather matching;

Visual

practical.

Test scenes: - Ashcroft Cottage in ordinary daytime; - Cottage Garden
with Mara present; - Village Green in rain; - snow state; - night

Review questions: - Is prose still primary? - Does artwork support
rather than overwhelm text? - Is character identity consistent? - Is
architecture consistent? - Is weather immediately legible? - Is text
readable over every scene treatment? - Does mobile cropping preserve
important subjects? - Does ordinary Bellwether feel inviting? - Are
horror-capable visual systems present without making normal life ominous
too early?

Part 2 is complete only when:

2.  The Asset Bible exists.

After Part 2 is approved, Part 3 can scale presentation into functional
game interfaces:

-   dynamic explored-world map;
-   gardening panel;
-   recipe book;
-   cottage restoration/home view;
-   interpretive relationship panel;
-   investigation notebook;
-   inventory presentation refinement;
-   weather ambience;
-   ecological ambience;
-   location ambience;
-   initial audio layering.

Part 4 then handles: - adaptive atmosphere; - state-driven UI
corruption; - recurrence presentation; - severe horror presentation; -
responsive polish; - accessibility basics such as reduced motion; -
visual regression review; - long-session presentation testing; - final
v0.3.0 certification.

CLOSING VISUAL PRINCIPLE

Bellwether’s visuals should make the player want to live there before
they make the player afraid of what lives there.

A cottage should become familiar enough that a changed window is
disturbing.

A garden should become loved enough that an impossible flower matters.

A face should become recognizable enough that the wrong expression
creates unease.

A road should become known enough that a changed turning feels
impossible.

The visual system must therefore serve the same central design principle
as the simulation:

First establish a coherent, comforting, learnable normality.

Then let Bellwether violate it deliberately.

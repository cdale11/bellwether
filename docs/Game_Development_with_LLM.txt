BELLWETHER — GAME DEVELOPMENT WITH LLM
Roadmap and recommended development sequence, 2026-07-08

ROADMAP FROM v0.3.4 TO v1.0
v0.3.5 — Baseline certification: diagnostics, packaging, metadata, asset consistency, clean baseline.
v0.4.0 — Living cottage and daily routine: gardening, cooking, restoration, home routines, weather/season interaction, satisfying ordinary weeks.
v0.4.1 — Economy and work: jobs, employment paths, business pressures, supply chains, selling goods, village economic consequences.
v0.4.2 — Hobbies, skills, lifestyle: selected subset of fishing, foraging, research, birdwatching, photography, music, volunteering, collecting or crafting.
v0.5.0 — Complete core cast: expand toward 6–7 deeply simulated major NPCs with authored lives, goals, memories and personal arcs.
v0.5.1 — Village population: roughly 15–20 lighter persistent residents with cheaper simulation.
v0.5.2 — Relationship depth: promises, favours, invitations, conflict, resentment, repair, trust and optional romance foundations.
v0.6.0 — World expansion: woods, farms, quarry, caves and a somewhat larger village.
v0.6.1 — Better travel: familiar-route shortcuts, first-time observations, weather effects, meetings, discoveries and encounters.
v0.7.0 — Town Mind: hierarchical LLM intelligence coordinating village-scale developments.
v0.7.1 — NPC cognition and memory: long-term beliefs, impressions, concerns, memories and bounded retrieval.
v0.7.2 — Procedural social arcs: grounded multi-day situations and errands emerging from real game state.
v0.8.0 — Full mystery expansion: interconnected evidence, testimony, records, ecology, geography and institutional investigation.
v0.8.1 — Adaptive horror: horror responding to player routine, relationships, behaviour and accumulated knowledge.
v0.8.2 — Interface horror: controlled map contradictions, altered text, portrait changes, journal inconsistencies and other state-driven presentation effects.
v0.9.0 — Recurrence expansion: fragmented memory return, asymmetric NPC memory, anchoring and richer cross-run consequences.
v0.9.1 — Failure and recovery: better injury, setbacks, recovery, preparation checks and authored death routes.
v1.0 RC1 — Complete story: full authored narrative from arrival to ending eligibility.
v1.0 RC2 — Ending families: Incorporation, False Escape, Rupture, Accommodation, Containment and True Liberation-Coexistence.
v1.0 RC3 — Postgame: farming, jobs, relationships, hobbies, side mysteries and changed village state after resolution.
v1.0 — Certification: audio, accessibility, performance, distribution, full playtesting and release certification.

Parallel workstreams: visual asset production, content authoring, and testing/performance work.

RECOMMENDED SEQUENCE
The immediate next build is v0.3.5, not v0.4.0. It is a focused engineering release: investigate the approved-art diagnostic mismatch, run the complete diagnostic suite, synchronize version metadata and stale headings, audit the asset manifest, verify save/load and migration, verify the map, verify free dialogue and deterministic fallback, package handoff and roadmap documents, update engineering context where needed, and produce a clean baseline. Keep gameplay changes out of v0.3.5.

After certification, v0.4.0 should ask: Can someone enjoy living in Bellwether for a full in-game week without touching the mystery?

Target ordinary-life loop:
wake -> inspect weather -> tend garden -> cook or eat -> work/errand/social visit -> return home -> restore/read/cook/write/rest -> persistent consequences tomorrow

MEMORY FOUNDATION SEQUENCING ADJUSTMENT
Begin the structured event-memory substrate around v0.5.0 when the full core cast arrives, rather than waiting until v0.7.1. Relationships, promises, personal arcs, and offscreen social history need durable memory before full Town Mind implementation.

Practical sequence:
v0.3.5 certification -> v0.4.x ordinary-life foundation -> v0.5.0 core cast + memory substrate -> v0.5.1 population tiers -> v0.5.2 social consequences using that memory substrate -> v0.6.x world expansion -> v0.7.x full Town Mind, cognition and procedural arcs.

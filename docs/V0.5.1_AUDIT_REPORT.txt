BELLWETHER v0.5.1 AUDIT REPORT

Scope: Village Population and Simulation Tiers.

Implemented:
- 24 persistent lightweight residents with strict schema validation.
- Stable household, occupation, routine, trait and interest data.
- Batched hourly background movement and activity simulation.
- Cheap co-location social-link formation.
- UI presence integration without adding lightweight residents to expensive core-NPC AI loops.
- Safe migration from older saves.

Architecture decision:
Lightweight residents are deliberately stored under population.residents rather than state.npcs. The latter remains the deeply simulated core cast and is used by core social-web, knowledge, conversation, and Director systems. This prevents population growth from multiplying LLM calls and pairwise social simulation costs.

Known limitations:
- Lightweight residents do not yet support full free-form player dialogue. They are visible village inhabitants and simulation participants, not core conversational agents.
- Their social simulation is intentionally cheap and currently records bounded links rather than rich belief/relationship dimensions.
- Businesses and jobs can reference the broader population in later releases; v0.5.1 establishes the substrate without inventing unsupported economic mutations.

No known release-blocking defects after final test pass.

# Part 13 — Player Psychology and Evolving Identity

Part 13 adds a bounded interpretation layer over authoritative player history. Repeated behaviour, relationships, investigation, work, recovery and supernatural exposure shape trait tendencies, coping style and the town's social reading of the player.

The layer does not assign a fixed personality at character creation and does not rewrite player history. It derives an inspectable snapshot from existing state and records meaningful changes in identity interpretation. Dialogue receives a bounded public identity context so NPC reactions can increasingly reflect who the player has become.

Current traits: care, community, inquiry, routine, social, independence, resilience and avoidance. Current coping styles include unformed, connection, practical routine, self reliance, pattern seeking and withdrawal.

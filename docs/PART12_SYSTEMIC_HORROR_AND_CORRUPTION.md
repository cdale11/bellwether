# Part 12 — Systemic Horror and Corruption of Normality

Part 12 introduces an authored anomaly catalogue and a bounded runtime corruption layer. Horror remains gated by learned normality: story entry, ordinary activity history, and familiarity with multiple places. Eligible anomalies target existing systems such as geography, routines, architecture, transport, memory, ecology, and information.

An experienced anomaly creates a temporary, inspectable supernatural overlay in the shared world model while preserving a permanent experience log and the legacy psychological consequences used by the frozen baseline. Overlay expiry restores ordinary world state; it does not erase memory of the event.

The LLM and Directors cannot invent anomaly IDs or mutate the authored catalogue. Later Parts may react to experienced anomalies and overlays through psychology, danger, and recurrence systems.

BELLWETHER v1.3.0 SOCIETY AND GENERATIONAL TIME AUDIT

Scope reviewed before implementation: backend core simulation modules, AI provider/runtime/player paths, population and social systems, life simulation, economy and jobs, procedural content, full diagnostics, frontend action surfaces, documentation, content catalogues and legacy diagnostic suite.

Key findings:
1. v1.2.0 population residents were persistent and scheduled, but lacked a slow authoritative society layer summarising households, employment composition, social connectivity, isolation and migration pressure.
2. Lightweight residents were visible but had no inexpensive ordinary player-contact action.
3. AI tester failures in cooking/community were consistent with missing multi-stage and schedule/location prerequisite bias; action ranking was expanded without adding verbose context.
4. Diagnostic reports needed more separated quality measures and society-specific telemetry.
5. UI clutter can indirectly reduce both human and AI-player performance when the same public action surface exposes many low-relevance actions. The AI player consumes backend legal actions rather than pixels, so visual clutter itself is not causal, but action-surface clutter and weak progressive disclosure affect both.

Implementation principles: deterministic authority for demographic/economic facts; LLM interpretation remains bounded; no authored-story automation; no hidden demographic event may invalidate saves or hard-block progression.

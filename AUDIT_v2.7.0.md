# Bellwether v2.7.0 Audit — Resistance, Recovery & Counterplay

## Evidence-first findings
Before editing, the release audited `failure_recovery_model.py`, `horror_aftermath_model.py`, `town_mind_model.py`, danger handling, daily ticks, public action dispatch, property, enterprise, transport, relationship and investigation integrations.

Confirmed existing foundations: bounded terminal-failure recovery routes; preparation checks for hazards; adaptive failure pressure; ordinary-life strain recovery from tea, gardening, walking, fishing and hobbies; daily NPC aftermath recovery; cottage repair; business reinvestment; vehicle servicing; authored relationship repair; investigation evidence and contradiction tracking; and v2.6 persistent town strategies.

Confirmed gap: these recovery systems were local to their own domains. There was no authoritative player counterplay layer that could observe the active town strategy, prepare across domains, protect a chosen vulnerability, accumulate readiness/resilience, or deliberately reduce the leverage of an active pressure strategy.

## v2.7.0 implementation
Added `backend/core/resistance_model.py` with persistent readiness, resilience, protected domains, recovery/counterplay history and pressure absorption. Added public actions for home preparation, diversification, documentation, social grounding, deliberate recovery and prepared counteraction. Town pressure now consults a bounded resistance modifier before applying consequences. Existing authored truth remains authoritative: counterplay changes leverage and consequences, not canon or mystery facts.

## Deliberate non-changes
Existing failure recovery, horror aftermath, property repair, enterprise recovery, transport servicing, relationship progression and investigation were not replaced. No universal immunity system was added; protection expires and resilience only bounds pressure. No story facts or endings are generated by this layer.

## Verification boundary
Focused deterministic certification exercises state migration, preparation, protection, documentation, support, recovery, counteraction, public actions, public view exposure, town-pressure integration and bounded logs. Long autonomous Ollama play and browser interaction are not claimed by this audit.
